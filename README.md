# NYCDOE Social Studies Study Hub

An interactive, single-file "one-pager" web app that serves as a complete, offline
learning and test-prep repository for **NYC Public School (NYCDOE) Social Studies**.
It replaces the need for a textbook and runs by simply double-clicking `index.html`.

Built for **Grade 7 Honors (United States & New York State History)** and expanding
to **Grade 8 (U.S. History from Reconstruction to the present)**.

## Why this exists

Middle school students need a calm, focused place to study and prep for tests
without ads, logins, or an internet connection. This app puts the full course
content, a chronological timeline, a searchable glossary, the NYS Social Studies
Practices, and a robust practice-question engine into one clean, phone-friendly file.

## Features

- **Runs 100% offline** — one `index.html` with inline CSS and vanilla JavaScript. No frameworks, no libraries, no CDN calls, no build step.
- **Tabbed navigation** — Home, Units, Timeline, Glossary, Skills, Practice & Tests, and Resources.
- **Five expandable units** — each with a focus question, "Big Picture" summary, key terms, key people, a dated timeline, and "Why It Matters / NYS Connection" notes.
- **Live search** — filter terms and topics across every unit, plus a searchable A–Z glossary.
- **NYS Social Studies Practices** — plain-English lessons on all six practices, with honors-level "try this" tasks and guidance on reading primary sources, maps, political cartoons, and attacking a DBQ.
- **Practice & Tests engine** — quiz-by-unit, Random Mix, and a cumulative Practice Final; instant feedback with explanations, a running score, and a results summary showing which units to review.
- **Progress saved locally** — scores persist between sessions using `localStorage` (works offline).
- **Accessible & responsive** — mobile-first layout, sticky header, 44px touch targets, 16px+ text, high contrast, semantic HTML, keyboard-navigable tabs, and no emojis.

## Question types (Regents-style)

- Stimulus-based multiple choice (quote, map, chart, or political-cartoon description)
- Standard multiple-choice content recall
- Constructed-response / short-answer prompts with model answers revealed on click
- DBQ-style practice with a short document excerpt, scaffolded questions, and a model response

> **Disclaimer:** All questions are original and copy no copyrighted test items. There is no
> separate standalone Grade 7 or Grade 8 Regents exam; these questions mirror the NYS
> assessment style students will face in later Regents courses.

## Grade coverage

1. Native Americans, Early Encounters, and Colonial Development
2. The Road to Independence
3. A New Nation: The United States Constitution
4. A Nation Grows: Expansion and Reform, 1800–1860
5. A Nation Divided (through the Civil War)

### Grade 8 — United States History (in progress)
1. Reconstruction
2. The Rise of American Industry & the Gilded Age
3. The Progressive Era & American Imperialism
4. Prosperity and Depression: The 1920s and the New Deal
5. The United States in an Age of Global Crisis (World Wars & the Holocaust)
6. The Cold War, Civil Rights, and a Changing Nation
